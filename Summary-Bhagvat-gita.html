Welcome to the Book: Bhagavad Gita

Chapter 1: Arjuna Vishada Yoga (Arjuna's Sorrow)

Arjuna feels deeply troubled and confused seeing his family and teachers on both sides of the battlefield. Overcome with sorrow, he puts down his bow and refuses to fight.

Chapter 2: Sankhya Yoga (Knowledge of the Soul)

Krishna explains that the soul never dies. He tells Arjuna to do his duty without worrying about the results.

Chapter 3: Karma Yoga (Path of Action)

Krishna says everyone must act according to their role. Doing your duty without being attached to results is best.

Chapter 4: Jnana Karma Sannyasa Yoga (Wisdom and Action)

: Krishna talks about how spiritual knowledge is passed down. He explains how wisdom and action together lead to growth.

Chapter 5: Karma Sannyasa Yoga (True Renunciation)

Krishna says giving up desire, not work, is true peace. Keep working, but do it as service.

Chapter 6: Dhyana Yoga (Meditation)

This chapter teaches how to meditate. It covers posture, breath control, and focus.

Chapter 7: Jnana Vijnana Yoga (Knowing God)

Krishna explains his nature and how devotion helps one know him.

Chapter 8: Akshara Brahma Yoga (Eternal Truth)

Describes what happens after death and how thinking of Krishna leads to good destiny.

Chapter 9: Raja Vidya Raja Guhya Yoga (Most Secret Knowledge)

Krishna reveals that he is everywhere and that loving him is the best path.

Chapter 10: Vibhuti Yoga (Krishna's Greatness)

Krishna tells Arjuna about his special powers and how he is present in the best things in the world.

Chapter 11: Vishwarupa Darshana Yoga (Vision of God's Form)

Krishna shows his huge, universal form. Arjuna sees everything and feels amazed.

Chapter 12: Bhakti Yoga (Path of Love)

Talks about the best kind of devotion. Simple love and faith are better than rituals.

Chapter 13: Kshetra Kshetrajna Vibhaga Yoga (Body and Soul)

Krishna explains the difference between the body and the soul.

Chapter 14: Gunatraya Vibhaga Yoga (Three Qualities)

Talks about three qualities that affect behavior: goodness, passion, and laziness.

Chapter 15: Purushottama Yoga (The Supreme Person)

Krishna talks about the tree of life and how to go beyond the material world.

Chapter 16: Daivasura Sampad Vibhaga Yoga (Good and Bad Qualities)

Explains the qualities of good (divine) and bad (demonic) people.

Chapter 17: Sraddhatraya Vibhaga Yoga (Types of Faith)

Chapter 18: Moksha Sannyasa Yoga (Freedom and Giving Up)

Krishna summarizes all teachings. He says doing your duty with love and no selfishness leads to freedom.
